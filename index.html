From Curiosity to QA: My Story

Ever since I was a child, I’ve always been a “researcher” with a natural curiosity. I’m the kind of person who loves to ask questions and dive deep into everything, from the simplest things to the most complex. One day, a friend of mine suggested that I should consider studying and specializing in QA, and being the curious person I am, I looked into it and realized it really suited my qualities. I enrolled in classes, and here I am!

During my studies, I discovered just how perfect the QA field is for me, where I can combine my love for questions, deep dives, and thorough exploration. I learned that software testing is much more than just a technical task; it’s an opportunity to understand each step in a system, think creatively about scenarios, and ensure the product meets all expectations—and even exceeds them. Every little detail and every question that uncovers an issue or improves the product represents a success that enhances the quality of the final outcome

I believe that qualities like curiosity, asking questions, and a relentless desire to explore are critical in QA work. The process of finding bugs and improving product quality requires not just knowledge, but also a genuine willingness to discover and investigate deeply, which is exactly what I bring to the field. I’m excited to continue learning, delving deeper, and contributing to the world of QA with my skills.

Why I Fit in QA

Attention to Detail

I’m good at breaking problems down, looking into them closely, and finding out what’s causing them. In QA, this helps me find bugs, examine different processes, ask the right questions, and suggest fixes that can make the product more stable and better. My love for solving problems and my analytical thinking are great tools in the QA world.
